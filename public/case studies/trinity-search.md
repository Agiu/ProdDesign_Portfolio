## Overview | ==Search is the shortcut students actually want==

When students can't find something through navigation, they reach for the search bar. At a liberal arts university, that search bar sits on top of an enormous, unevenly maintained pile of content — course catalogs, department pages, forms, news, event listings — all competing for the same query.

![](https://media.kaelub.com/TrinitySearch/1.png)

## The Problem

Site search on university websites is notoriously bad, and Trinity's is no exception. Results often surface outdated pages, PDFs, or news articles ahead of the actual page a student is looking for. This case study looks at why that happens and what a better search experience could look like for a school this size.

## Research & Process

This is a look into how students actually search — the phrasing they use, the number of tries before giving up, and where they land instead (Google, a professor's email, a Discord server) when the site's own search fails them.
