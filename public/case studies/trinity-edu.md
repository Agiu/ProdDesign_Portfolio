## Overview | ==Who is Trinity.edu actually built for?==

Trinity University's website, like most university sites, has to serve a lot of masters at once: admissions, donors, faculty, the board, alumni. Somewhere in that list, the day-to-day student — the person who's already enrolled and just needs to get something done — tends to fall to the bottom.

![](https://media.kaelub.com/TrinityEdu/1.png)

## The Problem

As a student, I kept noticing the same pattern: the pages that mattered most to me day-to-day (registration, advising, financial aid, housing) were harder to find than the pages built for prospective students and donors. The site's information architecture reflects the university's org chart more than it reflects how students actually think about their own needs.

## Research & Process

This case study walks through an audit of Trinity.edu from a current-student lens — mapping the tasks students actually come to the site to do, comparing that against the site's existing navigation and content priorities, and proposing a structure that centers student tasks first.
