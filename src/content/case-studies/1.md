## Overview

The Xbox platform restructuring aimed to untangle a notoriously complex dashboard that users found overwhelming. We set out to improve discoverability for games and media apps by adopting a flatter, more modular architecture.

My primary focus was defining a brutalist yet functional design system that embraced the "dark mode" aesthetic of gaming while optimizing for legibility across 10-foot TV interfaces and mobile companions. 

## The Challenge

Millions of daily active users rely on the Xbox interface to connect, play, and watch. Historical analytics showed that users were getting lost in deep navigational trees. Core challenges included:
- **Information Density:** Too many competing elements on the home screen.
- **Latency:** Heavy UI treatments causing noticeable structural lag.
- **Inconsistent Patterns:** Legacy menus using entirely different visual semantics.

## Our Approach

1. **Audit & Deprecate:** We ruthlessly cut features that saw less than 1% daily engagement.
2. **Modular Components:** Developed a universal card system that could stretch and reflow without losing its structural integrity.
3. **Typography Scaling:** Redefined the typographic scale to ensure perfect legibility at a distance, heavily leaning into **strong sans-serif** fonts for body data and expressive serifs for brand moments.

> "The redesign not only sped up navigation by 40% but fundamentally changed how our community interacts with content discovery."
> — Xbox Leadership Team

## Key Outcomes

The result was a unified dashboard where content breathes. We reduced time-to-first-interaction by significant margins and saw a profound increase in media consumption. 

*   **+40%** user task completion speed.
*   **-15%** reduction in support tickets related to navigation.
*   **Seamless** light-to-dark mode transitions implemented natively.
